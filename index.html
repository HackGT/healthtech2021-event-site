about

HackGT is proud to present HealthTech- a Hackathon centered around the wellbeing of individuals. Participants of this event will find solutions to a variety of problems that affect everyday health. We've partnered with organizations and nonprofits to create custom challenges that you can tackle! Think you have what it takes to help millions of people around the world? Apply today!

tracks images will go here

Mental Health

Mental health problems are some of the most common issues that are present throughout the world today. These problems can range from dealing with stress to certain disorders that affect daily lifestyles. Within this track, you will design solutions that improve the lifestyle of those affected by mental health issues.

Inpatient Health

It is essential that inpatients have the best possible experience during their stay in hospitals. In order to achieve this, hospitals must maintain the upmost standards in terms of sanitation, efficiency, and service. Participants within this track will design solutions that help keep these standards in the hospital.

Fitness & Nutrition

Maintaining a fitness routine is essential to living a healthy life. What's equally as important is that individuals must fuel their body with the right nutrients in order to keep their bodies in optimal shape; however, many people today face obstacles that keep them from receiving the necessary food. Within this track, participants will find ways to create a food and nutrition narrative: a new paradigm or a remaking of old ones that prioritizes and respects the central position of healthy, tasty, fair food in the human experience.

Women's Health

Women's health is often stigmatized and overlooked in numerous fields of health. They require different medical needs from men, and the PCOS Awareness organization is looking to bridge the gap and raise awareness. Participants in this track will help find ways to raise this awareness and to look for better ways to serve women's medical needs.

Global/Rural Health

Access to healthcare is limited in rural and less developed areas around the country and the world. It is important to expand access to quality healthcare in these areas, which could mean developing new technologies and infrastructure to expand the range of current medical services. Participants within this track will design solutions that increase access to healthcare, and/or find ways to make healthcare more affordable.

faq

  • What is a hackathon?
    A hackathon is an invention marathon where people work in groups to build hardware and software projects ranging from simple websites to motion controlled drones. At this hackathon, you will be working in teams of up to four people to create projects within our tracks [insert hyperlink of tracks].
  • What is HealthTech?
    Health Tech is modeled after the tech industry’s “hackathon” and aims to foster collaboration among students from various disciplines by uniting them for their passion within the healthcare fields. Students will work with nonprofit organizations to solve pressing problems — from mental health to geriatrics – faced by these groups in order to make a tangible impact within in their community.
  • What if I don't have a team?
    We will have a virtual mixer at the start of the event to help you find a team! Additionally, we will be making a Facebook group for attendees to share their ideas and find a team before the event.
  • How long is HealthTech?
    Health Tech will begin Saturday, March 13, 2021 and will end Sunday, March 14, 2021.
  • Where is it?
    The event will be held virtually.
  • Is there anyting I need?
    Make sure to have a laptop, charger, headphones, and lots of snacks.
  • Who can participate?
    Any student over the age of 18 is allowed to participate. Teams can be up to four members - you can register with your team when you apply, any time after you're accepted, or create a team at the event! If you are younger than 18, but a Georgia Tech student, you can still participate.
  • What kind of materials or hardware will be provided?
    Unfortunately, since the event will be virtual we will be unable to provide any materials or hardware, but you are welcome to use anything you have access to.
